Known Data Units and Conversion

  • Bit

    1 Bit is the smallest unit of data and represents a binary digit (0 or 1).

    8 Bit = 1 Byte.

    Bit to Byte, divide by 8.

  • Byte

    1 Byte is equal to 8 Bit. It is commonly used to represent a character in a computer system.

    1 KiloByte = 1024 Byte.

    Byte to KiloByte, divide by 1024.

  • KiloByte (KB)

    1 KiloByte is equal to 1024 Byte. It is often used to represent small files or data in computer storage.

    1 MegaByte = 1024 KiloByte.

    KiloByte to MegaByte, divide by 1024.

  • MegaByte (MB)

    1 MegaByte is equal to 1024 KiloByte. It is commonly used to represent larger files or data in computer storage.

    1 GigaByte = 1024 MegaByte.

    MegaByte to GigaByte, divide by 1024.

  • GigaByte (GB)

    1 GigaByte is equal to 1024 MegaByte. It is used to represent very large files or data in computer storage.

    1 TeraByte = 1024 GigaByte.

    GigaByte to TeraByte, divide by 1024.

Note: If you want to convert the other way around multiply instead of divide
